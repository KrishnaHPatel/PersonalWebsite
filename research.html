Weill Cornell Medicine

Research Intern - Summer 2023

Research Overview

Innovative spacesuit filtration system development and publication

I collaborated with Fremen Space Inc., a Cornell-based start-up, to develop a new, innovative filtration system for spacesuits. This research focused on addressing critical hygiene and hydration challenges faced by astronauts during long-duration spacewalks.

Our work resulted in a published research paper in Frontiers in Space Technology.

Technical Contributions

Key technical work and development contributions

Software Development

Developed software for controlling the filtration system using C++ and Arduino IDE, implementing real-time monitoring and control algorithms for the spacesuit filtration system.

Electronic Circuit Design

Designed and implemented electronic circuits for the filtration system, ensuring reliable operation and efficient power consumption in space environments.

CAD Modeling & Prototyping

Created detailed CAD models to prototype system components, enabling rapid iteration and testing of the filtration system design.
